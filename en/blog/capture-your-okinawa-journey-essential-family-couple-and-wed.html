Capture Your Okinawa Journey: Essential Family, Couple, and Wedding Photography with usher in making

usher in making is a female photographer team that has been capturing Okinawa's beauty since 2014.

This year, too, the popular snap shoot season of October-November has arrived, regardless of country or region.

Okinawa still feels like the height of summer, but October, with its pleasant weather perfect for swimming after the peak heat, brings many inquiries for family photo shoots.

Family photo shoots amidst Okinawa's coral and emerald seas, exotic plants, and rocky landscapes are popular. Since it's a close destination offering both relaxation and sightseeing, many also come for babymoon trips, capturing both maternity and family photos.

The photos below are of a three-generation family from Hong Kong.

This year, especially with the long Chuseok holiday, we've received many last-minute inquiries.

Even now, we're receiving daily bookings for #OkinawaFamilySnap and #OkinawaCoupleSnap from late September to the second week of October.

October and November are also popular months for main ceremony and wedding photo shoots, so if you're considering it, please inquire about bookings quickly~~

To maintain photo quality and account for weather changes like sudden downpours, we generally have a rule of accepting only up to two teams per day with a time gap between shoots.

However, October weather tends to be stable (the probability of typhoons, heavy rain warnings, or rain is significantly lower compared to Okinawa's weather from June to September).

On some busy days, we do accept bookings for about three teams for 1-hour shoots.

We also receive many inquiries for large family photo shoots of 8-10 people or more. We get many requests from family-oriented Asian countries like Korea, Japan, Taiwan, Hong Kong, and Singapore~

Since Okinawa is a close and relaxing destination, many three-generation families come for #Okinawa70thBirthdayTrips with their parents.

Please note that #OkinawaLargeFamilySnap is very popular.
